Why India Can Deploy This at National Scale

The infrastructure already exists. The missing layer is the right policy framework.

Many countries are discussing congestion management and incentive-based mobility policies. Few have the digital infrastructure required to operate them at scale. India does. Over the past decade, India has built one of the world's most advanced public digital infrastructures.

These systems make it possible to manage road demand and commuter incentives at national scale. MTSAi is designed to operate on top of that infrastructure.

Aadhaar

Secure Identity at Scale

Secure identity verification for over a billion residents, enabling trusted digital participation in public programmes.

UPI

Real-Time Payments

Real-time digital payments at massive scale, making it possible to distribute incentives directly to commuters. More than 20 billion transactions are processed every month.

FASTag

Nationwide Electronic Tolling

Nationwide electronic tolling connecting more than 8 crore vehicles to road infrastructure, allowing automated vehicle identification without additional roadside hardware.

ICCCs

Smart City Command Centres

Integrated Command and Control Centres are operational across multiple Smart Cities, providing real-time urban traffic monitoring and coordination infrastructure.

Together, these systems form a digital foundation that few countries possess.

The Missing Layer: Demand Management

While India has built extraordinary infrastructure, urban congestion continues to grow in major cities. Road capacity alone cannot solve the problem. Congestion is ultimately driven by when and how people choose to travel.

Demand-management tools help cities influence those choices. MTSAi is designed specifically to provide that layer using what India has already built.

India built the infrastructure. MTSAi provides the demand layer.

Why India Can Lead

India has repeatedly demonstrated its ability to deploy digital public infrastructure at unprecedented scale. Aadhaar, UPI, and FASTag have transformed the way public systems operate. The next step is applying that same digital capability to urban mobility.

By aligning commuter incentives with the public good, India has the opportunity to pioneer a new model of congestion management — one that other countries will study and follow.
