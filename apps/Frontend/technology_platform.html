Platform Architecture Overview

MULTI-TENANT IUDM PLATFORM

Incentive-Based Urban Demand Management Platform

Urban congestion management requires more than isolated tools. It requires an integrated system that connects policy configuration, traffic data, commuter engagement, and digital mobility ecosystems.

The MTSAi platform is designed as a multi-tenant mobility management architecture that allows multiple cities to operate independent congestion management programs within a shared infrastructure environment.

The platform combines deterministic policy engines, incentive management systems, and digital ecosystem integrations to support government-controlled traffic demand programs.

Architecture Focus

  • →Government-controlled policy configuration and audit logs
  • →Deterministic incentive and pricing services
  • →Event processing that leverages existing mobility infrastructure
  • →Super App integration pathways for incentive delivery
  • →Privacy and deployment posture designed for deployment, not live operation

1. City Policy and Operations Layer

GOVERNMENT CONTROL

At the top of the platform is the City Administration and Policy Layer. This is where government authorities manage congestion programs through a structured operational interface.

City administrators can configure congestion pricing policies, peak and off-peak definitions, incentive programs and reward budgets, exemption categories and equity protections, and enforcement thresholds and escalation rules.

Operational Controls

  • →Congestion pricing policies
  • →Peak and off-peak definitions
  • →Reward budgets and incentive programs
  • →Exemptions and equity protections
  • →Enforcement thresholds and escalation rules with complete audit logs

This layer ensures that policy authority remains entirely with the government authority, not the technology provider.

2. Core Platform Engines

DETERMINISTIC POLICY SERVICES

The operational core of the platform consists of several specialized services that execute congestion management policies.

Incentive Engine

The Incentive Engine evaluates commuter behavior against city-defined rules and calculates rewards when commuters adopt preferred travel behaviors.

  • • off-peak travel
  • • carpool participation
  • • public transit usage
  • • route optimization during congestion events

The engine distributes rewards through integrated digital mobility platforms.

Dynamic Pricing Engine

The pricing engine calculates congestion charges based on city-configured base rates, real-time congestion conditions, vehicle occupancy estimates, and policy rules such as caps, credits, and exemptions.

All calculations follow deterministic logic with full auditability, ensuring that operational decisions remain transparent and explainable.

Gamification and Engagement Layer

To support long-term commuter participation, the platform includes engagement systems such as leaderboard rankings, achievement badges, behavioral streak tracking, and community participation metrics.

These systems are designed to reinforce positive travel behavior without exposing personal identity data.

3. Data and Event Processing Layer

EVENT PIPELINE

The platform processes mobility events from multiple sources. These include ANPR/LPR vehicle detection systems, partner mobility applications, and trip start and end signals from integrated platforms.

Incoming events are normalized, validated, and processed through the platform’s event pipeline before being evaluated by the pricing and incentive engines.

Processing Objectives

  • →Normalize and validate events from multiple sources
  • →Support evaluation by pricing and incentive engines
  • →Leverage existing infrastructure rather than require new roadside hardware networks

4. Digital Mobility Ecosystem Integration

SUPER APP GATEWAY

A central feature of the platform is its ability to operate within existing digital mobility ecosystems. Through the MTSAi Super App integration gateway, partner platforms can receive incentive distribution events, display commuter engagement status, provide trip data signals, and surface congestion alerts and route guidance.

This allows commuter incentives to be delivered through platforms that citizens already use for mobility and payments.

Partner Interaction Model

  • →Incentive distribution events
  • →Commuter engagement status
  • →Trip data signals
  • →Congestion alerts and route guidance

5. Privacy and Data Governance Architecture

TENANT ISOLATION

Privacy Safeguards

  • check_circleNo continuous GPS tracking: the platform does not require persistent location surveillance to operate.
  • check_circleNo biometric data collection: biometric capture is not part of the system design.
  • check_circlePseudonymized commuter identifiers: engagement and event flows are designed around minimized identity exposure.

Governance Controls

  • check_circleTenant isolation: each city operates as an independent data tenant, ensuring that commuter data cannot be correlated across jurisdictions.
  • check_circleCity authority control: city authorities maintain control over data retention policies and access permissions.
  • check_circleDesign posture: these controls describe intended governance architecture designed for deployment, not an already-operational environment.

6. Infrastructure and Deployment

CLOUD POSTURE

The MTSAi platform is designed for deployment within cloud environments aligned with local regulatory requirements. For India deployments, the architecture supports data residency within the AWS Mumbai region, scalable cloud infrastructure, secure API integration with external systems, and infrastructure defined through version-controlled provisioning frameworks.

This architecture supports both initial pilot deployments and long-term city expansion.

Deployment Posture

  • →AWS Mumbai region data residency support
  • →Scalable cloud infrastructure
  • →Secure API integration with external systems
  • →Infrastructure defined through version-controlled provisioning frameworks

Government Evaluation Path

This platform architecture is designed to support evaluation, integration planning, and phased government deployment.
