Government Policy Path

A Policy Platform for Managing Urban Traffic Demand.

The platform provides tools enabling governments to manage congestion through incentive programs and configurable pricing policies. It is designed for deployment in a phased, auditable public-sector environment.

MTSAi introduces Incentive-Based Urban Demand Management (IUDM): a mobility demand management platform built around incentives, policy control, and public accountability rather than a penalty-first congestion system.

MTSAi is currently in a pre-deployment evaluation stage. The platform architecture is designed for phased implementation, controlled pilot deployment, and government-defined policy oversight.

Section 2 / Governance

Government Control Framework

Government authorities retain control over the public policy parameters of the system. MTSAi is designed to operate as infrastructure supporting those decisions, not as a substitute for them.

Authorities define how congestion pricing rules, incentive budgets, exemptions, equity protections, and enforcement thresholds are configured within a city programme.

Control Area 01

Congestion Pricing Rules

Zones, time bands, charging logic, and pricing conditions remain under government control.

Control Area 02

Incentive Budgets

Authorities determine which incentive programmes run, their budget constraints, and how rewards are structured.

Control Area 03

Exemptions and Equity Protections

Any exemptions, hardship controls, or equity protections remain policy decisions made by the city or state authority.

Control Area 04

Enforcement Thresholds

Authorities set the thresholds and conditions under which enforcement-linked actions are triggered inside the programme design.

Section 3 / Auditability

Transparency and Auditability

calculate

Pricing Calculations

The platform is designed so that pricing calculations are generated as traceable records rather than opaque outputs. This allows review against the underlying policy rules.

gavel

Enforcement Actions

Enforcement-linked actions are designed to create auditable records showing which rule was applied, under what condition, and through which operational process.

redeem

Reward Distributions

Reward distributions are designed to be recorded with enough detail for programme review, audit, and reconciliation against incentive policy settings.

account_balance

Public Accountability

This auditability posture is intended to support government accountability, procurement review, and the ability to explain programme operation in public-sector terms.

Section 4 / Privacy

Privacy Safeguards

The platform is designed around architectural protections intended to reduce surveillance risk and align public-sector mobility policy with data minimization principles.

No biometric data is collected. No continuous location tracking is required. Data collection is limited to what is needed to determine eligibility, distribute rewards, and support aggregated programme oversight.

  • ✗
    No biometric data: the platform is not designed around biometric identity systems.
  • ✗
    No continuous tracking: the system does not depend on continuous location surveillance.
  • ✓
    Data minimization: collection is limited to purpose-specific operational signals.
  • ✓
    Government as controller: city or state authority retains controller responsibility for programme data governance.

Section 6 / Delivery Model

Phased Implementation

The deployment model is designed to reduce perceived risk by moving through a phased structure rather than an immediate citywide programme launch.

The phases cover architecture development, system integration, pilot deployment, and city programme expansion. Each step is intended to make the next step more evidence-based and more manageable for the authority.

  • 01
    Architecture development: establish the policy and technical foundation.
  • 02
    System integration: connect the platform to city and ecosystem interfaces.
  • 03
    Pilot deployment: run a bounded programme before scale.
  • 04
    City programme expansion: extend only if evaluation supports continuation.
