Privacy Posture

MTSAi is designed to support notice and consent patterns where required, and to align with DPDP-informed data principal request workflows. This page summarises the privacy posture and what this website collects.

DPDP-Informed Posture

Designed to support notice and consent patterns where required.

Designed to support data principal request workflows (access/correction/erasure where applicable) as defined by contract and law.

Grievance channel is treated as a core operational requirement for trust.

Key Commitment

Privacy is a design constraint, not an afterthought. The system is architected to minimise personal data collection and maximise city authority control over what is collected and retained.

What This Website Collects
  • ✓ Contact details you provide (name, work email/phone, organisation)
  • ✓ Message content you submit (your request details)
  • ✓ Basic technical logs needed to protect and operate the site — scoped and retained per operational policy
Controlled Diligence (NDA/RFP)

The following are available under NDA or RFP process:

  • Data flow summaries (high-level)
  • Role definitions as contract-defined
  • Retention and deletion postures per deployment model
  • DPIA-style templates where applicable
Key Commitment

No personal data is shared with third parties for commercial purposes. Data Principal rights under DPDP Act 2023 are honoured: access, correction, erasure, and grievance redress.
