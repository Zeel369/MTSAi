01 // Platform Philosophy
"Cities don't need another penalty-based toll system. They need a platform that makes the right commuting choice feel like the obvious one."

Traditional congestion pricing programs rely heavily on penalties and infrastructure deployment. Cities increasingly require tools that influence commuter behavior while maintaining public trust and policy transparency.

Congestion is not only a road-capacity problem — it is a repeated coordination problem created by large numbers of commuters making similar route and timing decisions during peak periods.

03. The IUDM
Model

Incentive-Based Urban Demand Management allows cities to reward off-peak travel, encourage carpooling, support transit usage, and influence route choices. Gamification and digital incentives create sustained commuter engagement. The model is designed to shift behavior through participation rather than rely only on deterrence.

§09 · Frequently Asked

Common questions,
direct answers.

Straightforward answers about the IUDM platform, deployment approach, and partnership options.

IUDM is a demand-shaping mechanism that uses real-time digital incentives — such as rewards, cashbacks, and preferential routing — to redistribute mobility demand across corridors and time windows. Unlike pricing-based systems, IUDM works within a zero-revenue-extraction model: the government does not charge citizens; instead, behavioural shifts are rewarded. The goal is to reduce peak congestion by 25–40% without coercive measures.

MTSAi is built on a Privacy-by-Design foundation. All personally identifiable information (PII) is anonymised at the edge before it enters the analytics layer. The platform is fully compliant with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDPA) 2023 and aligns with GIGW 3.0 standards. No citizen data is sold, profiled, or monetised. Government retains full data sovereignty with audit-ready logs accessible via a dedicated Trust Centre dashboard.

The platform follows a phased risk-reduction approach:

  • Phase 1 · Mapping & Baselining — Data ingestion, corridor identification, demand baseline (Completed)
  • Phase 2 · Model Validation — IUDM simulation on synthetic and anonymised historical data (In Progress)
  • Phase 3 · Regulatory Alignment — DPDPA compliance audit, procurement documentation, MoU preparation
  • Phase 4 · Pilot Deployment — Live corridor trial with selected government partner (Target: Q2 2026)

Each phase must be signed off before the next begins. No deployment claims are made before Phase 4 is completed.

MTSAi has three distinct partnership tracks:

  • Government Bodies — Municipal corporations, State Transport Departments, Smart City SPVs. Engagement typically begins with an MoU followed by a funded pilot under the city's existing IT procurement framework.
  • Technology Partners — ERP integrators, ride-hailing platforms, payment aggregators, and urban data providers wishing to integrate with the IUDM API layer.
  • Strategic Investors — Impact investors and infrastructure funds aligned with sustainable urban mobility. Investment structuring is handled via formal due diligence and NDA.

Yes. MTSAi is designed as an integration-first platform. It exposes RESTful APIs and webhook streams compatible with state-run mobility apps (e.g., ONDC, city bus apps, metro apps), existing ERP/EIS systems, and NPCI-aligned payment rails. The Super App layer can be deployed as a white-label module within a city's existing app — no new citizen-facing app download is required. The platform also supports data ingestion from IoT traffic sensors, GPS transponders, and FASTag infrastructure.

Traditional congestion pricing is a punitive, revenue-extraction model — citizens are charged more to use roads during peak hours. This generates political resistance and disproportionately impacts lower-income commuters. IUDM inverts this logic: instead of penalising peak usage, it rewards off-peak or alternative route choices. The government spends on incentives rather than collecting penalties, making the model politically viable, socially equitable, and behaviourally more effective.
