Policy Architecture

Four Policy Controls.

01 // Pricing Rules

Pricing Rules

City authorities configure programme rules, corridor logic, and decision thresholds. The incentive engine operates strictly within the boundaries defined by the city's policy configuration — no autonomous rule changes occur without explicit government approval.

Corridor Thresholds Time Windows Zone Logic
02 // Incentive Structures

Incentive Structures

Reward categories, budgets, and eligibility thresholds are set through policy, not consumer defaults. The government defines who is eligible for incentives, what behaviours are rewarded, and what the total programme spend envelope is per billing cycle.

Reward Categories Budget Caps Eligibility Rules
03 // Exemptions

Exemptions

Equity protections and programme exclusions remain part of public policy control. Emergency vehicles, healthcare workers, and economically vulnerable commuters can be granted blanket exemptions through the Policy Console — no case-by-case vendor requests needed.

Category Exemptions Hardship Pathways Vehicle Classes
04 // Enforcement Thresholds

Enforcement Thresholds

Operational thresholds and response rules remain under city-level oversight. If a corridor exceeds a defined congestion threshold, the system escalates to human operators — it does not take autonomous enforcement action without explicit government-defined response parameters.

IUDM vs Traditional Pricing 10-Point Comparison · Policy Architecture
Criterion Traditional Congestion Pricing MTSAi IUDM Model
Citizen Experience Punitive — citizens pay penalties for peak travel Rewarding — citizens earn incentives for better choices
Political Viability High resistance — perceived as a "tax on commuters" Positive framing — rewards-based, not punishment-based
Revenue Model Revenue extraction from citizens Zero revenue extraction — government spends on incentives
Equity Impact Disproportionate burden on lower-income commuters Built-in exemptions and hardship pathways by design
Infrastructure Dependency Requires gantry hardware, RFID, or camera networks Software-first; integrates with existing FASTag & UPI rails
Data Privacy Often requires continuous vehicle tracking Edge-anonymised signals — no continuous GPS tracking
Government Control Varies — often delegated to private operators City authority is controller; MTSAi is processor only
Auditability Limited — black-box pricing in many implementations Full audit trail with government-accessible logs
Deployment Speed 18–36 months typical (hardware procurement) Software overlay — pilot-ready within existing app ecosystems
Behaviour Mechanism Deterrence — avoidance of cost Positive reinforcement — participation through reward
